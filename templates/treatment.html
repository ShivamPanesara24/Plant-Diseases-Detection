Plant Disease Treatment Guide

Find out what treatment each plant needs!

Apple Scab

Apple Scab is a fungal disease caused by Venturia inaequalis. It infects leaves, fruits, and young shoots, leading to dark, scaly lesions, premature leaf drop, and reduced fruit quality.

More Details

Apple Cedar Rust

Apple Cedar Rust is a fungal disease caused by Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae. It affects apple trees and junipers, causing orange spots on leaves and fruit, leading to defoliation and reduced yield.

More Details

Black Rot

Black Rot is a fungal disease caused by Botryosphaeria obtusa. It affects apple trees, leading to circular brown spots on leaves, shriveled fruit, and cankers on branches.

More Details

Corn – Cercospora Leaf Spot

Cercospora Leaf Spot, also known as Gray Leaf Spot, is a fungal disease in maize caused by Cercospora zeae-maydis.

More Details

Corn – Common Rust

Common Rust of corn is caused by the fungus Puccinia sorghi and leads to pustule formation on leaves.

More Details

Corn – Northern Leaf Blight

Northern Leaf Blight is a fungal disease caused by Exserohilum turcicum, creating elongated lesions on maize leaves.

More Details

Corn – Healthy

Healthy corn shows no disease symptoms and produces strong, green, high-yielding plants.

More Details

Black Rot

Black Rot is a fungal disease caused by Guignardia bidwellii, creating dark lesions on grapes.

More Details

Esca (Black Measles)

Esca is a fungal disease caused by Phaeoacremonium and Phaeomoniella, causing necrotic lesions on grapevines.

More Details

Leaf Blight (Isariopsis Leaf Spot)

Leaf Blight is a fungal disease caused by Isariopsis capensis, creating angular lesions on grape leaves.

More Details

Healthy Grapes

Healthy grapes are free from diseases and pests, with vibrant leaves and unblemished fruit.

More Details

Bacterial Spot

Bacterial Spot is caused by Xanthomonas campestris, creating dark lesions on tomato plants.

More Details

Early Blight

Early Blight is a fungal disease caused by Alternaria solani, creating circular lesions on tomato plants.

More Details

Late Blight

Late Blight is caused by Phytophthora infestans, creating irregular, water-soaked lesions on tomato plants.

More Details

Leaf Mold

Leaf Mold is caused by Passalora fulva, leading to yellowing leaves and a grayish mold on the undersides.

More Details

Septoria Leaf Spot

Septoria Leaf Spot is caused by Septoria lycopersici, creating small, dark-bordered lesions on tomato leaves.

More Details

Spider Mites (Two-spotted Spider Mite)

Spider Mites cause stippling on tomato leaves, leading to yellow spots and webbing.

More Details

Target Spot

Target Spot is caused by Neofusicoccum spp., leading to dark lesions with light centers on tomato leaves.

More Details

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus causes yellowing and curling of tomato leaves, stunted growth, and yield reduction.

More Details

Tomato Mosaic Virus

Tomato Mosaic Virus causes mottling and stunted growth in tomato plants.

More Details
